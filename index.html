About Us

our story:

In 1989, British scientist Tim Berners-Lee, working at CERN, proposed a revolutionary idea: the World Wide Web. His goal was to create a universal system for sharing information across computers, making it easier for scientists to collaborate globally.

Initially, the web was a tool for researchers to exchange data, but its potential quickly became evident. In 1993, CERN made the technology free and open to everyone, igniting a global transformation.

From that single, modest website, the web grew into a vast network of billions of sites, changing how we communicate, learn, and share ideas. Today’s internet, with its dynamic, multimedia-rich websites, owes its existence to Berners-Lee’s vision—a simple idea that reshaped the modern world.
